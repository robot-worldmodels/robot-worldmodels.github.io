Robot World Models

RSS 2026 Workshop

World models have emerged as predictive tools that simulate environment dynamics in a robotâ€™s action space, allowing agents to anticipate the outcomes of their actions. These learned models have become essential for decision-making and long-horizon planning in embodied agents, enabling robots to reason about future states and plan complex behaviors. This workshop brings together an interdisciplinary community spanning generative modeling, model-based reinforcement learning, neural rendering, and differentiable simulation to advance the state of the art in world models for robotics. By focusing on embodied decision-making, we aim to push world models beyond passive prediction towards active, goal-driven interaction and control in both simulated and real environments.

We are sourcing two different types of papers: four-page papers and one-page abstracts, focusing on robot world models. Read the submission page for more details.

Topics

  • Action-conditioned world models
  • Model-based reinforcement learning and long-horizon planning
  • Differentiable simulation for control and decision-making
  • Learning dynamics and physical interactions from data
  • Sim-to-real transfer and robustness
  • Evaluation of world models in robotics contexts
